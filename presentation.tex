  clustered around discrete events (protected discussion time, training, the
  Platform team's Kafka failure-handling session) --- what the thesis calls
  "event-anchored" use. The dashboard shows the resulting backlog (13
  hypotheses, validated/open counts). Key enabler: delivering ArchHypo inside
  Backstage, where teams already work, lowered context-switching cost ---
  named explicitly by participants as a facilitating factor.%

  uncertainty, which we abstracted into new patterns.\par
  \medskip

  compliance-driven and shared-infrastructure concerns; Reliability and
  Auditability dominated the quality-attribute distribution, reflecting the
  regulated study site. From thematic analysis we named five emerging
  structural patterns (Group B). They complement the existing ArchHypo pattern
  language: prior patterns described HOW hypotheses are assessed/deferred;
  these describe WHAT KIND of hypothesis is being assessed. Stress this is
  nominative --- a vocabulary for recognition, not a frequency claim, given the
  dataset size.%

% ============================================================================
% Slide 8 — Implications / lessons learned   [RQ3]
% ============================================================================
% SCRIPT: "Distributed architectural work needs lightweight structures that
%          preserve team autonomy while maintaining shared decision quality."
\begin{frame}{Implications \& lessons learned}
  \vfill
  \begin{columns}[T]
    \begin{column}{0.31\textwidth}
      \begin{block}{Finding}
        Even with tool support, hypothesis work stayed \alert{senior-anchored}
        (12 of 13).
      \end{block}
    \end{column}
    \begin{column}{0.31\textwidth}
      \begin{block}{Implication}
        A tool removes \alert{friction} but cannot supply the
        \alert{organizational conditions} for broad participation.
      \end{block}
    \end{column}
    \begin{column}{0.31\textwidth}
      \begin{block}{Lesson}
        Ship hypothesis support \alert{inside} the developer portal ---
        \emph{extend} the workflow, don't add a new one.
      \end{block}
    \end{column}
  \end{columns}
  \vfill
  \centering\small
  Distributed architectural work needs lightweight structure that preserves
  team autonomy while protecting shared decision quality.
  \vfill
\end{frame}
\note{%
  Script: The broader lesson is that distributed architectural work needs
  lightweight structures that preserve team autonomy while maintaining shared
  decision quality.\par
  \medskip
  Speaker notes: RQ3 framed as finding -> implication -> lesson. For
  practitioners: embedding Hypo-Stage in Backstage removed adoption friction
  (consistent with Team Topologies: tooling should follow team cognitive
  pathways). But tool availability is necessary, not sufficient --- broad
  participation also needs protected architectural time, statement-level
  scaffolding, and a recurring backlog-review ceremony (the Group A
  organizational patterns). For researchers: formative evaluation plus
  artifact analysis of the tool's own data was a productive combination for
  workflow-coupled artifacts.%
}
